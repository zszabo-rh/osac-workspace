Summary
Policy updates dominated the meeting with focus on human review necessity for AI code and immediate compliance deadlines.

Policy Document Style and Scope
Feedback indicated the Agentic Life Cycle Policy needed a more aggressive adoption tone, emphasizing style over substance. There was strong consensus to create a separate document outlining best practices for using agentic tools within the repository.
[a]
Human Review for AI Code
The team agreed that the current policy must require at least 1 or 2 human reviews for code changes, asserting that relying solely on AI for code writing and acceptance is questionable. This stance is based on the technical risk of massive, human-unreadable AI-generated changes, emphasizing the policy must be treated as a living document.

IP Management and MOC 2.0
The team needs to structure APIs and assign responsibilities for public IP management, starting with a Metal LB implementation as a prioritized use case. The OSAC team must be included in the development cluster of the first MOC 2.0 build by June 1st, with the larger compliance goal being HIPPA and NIST 800-171 certification by the end of the summer.
