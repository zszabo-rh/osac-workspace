Summary
Discussion focused on bare metal as a service enhancement proposal with ensuring future ACM compatibility and validating VM as a service for MOC 2.0 readiness.

OSAC ESI, Floating IP Use
OSAC will continue using OpenStack/ESI for the foreseeable future and allocates 2 floating IPs per cluster: 1 for the API address and 1 for the ingress IP. Requiring 2 IPs per cluster aligns with previous networking discussions regarding IPv4 requirements.

Bare Metal Service Approval
The Bare Metal as a Service enhancement proposal must proceed quickly to meet June deadlines while ensuring the design is not objectionable to the ACM team. It was decided that the architecture must result in a broadly useful solution that is not exclusively specific to OSAC and can converge with broader product direction.

VM as a Service Validation
VM as a service is not production-ready for MOC 2.0 due to unsolved operational problems like monitoring and charging. The proposed strategy is to deploy VM as a service to a development MOC environment as an optional service for non-production use, allowing teams to gain experience and validate the operational experience.
