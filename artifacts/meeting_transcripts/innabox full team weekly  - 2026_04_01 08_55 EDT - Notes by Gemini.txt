Summary
Metal Cube team alignment and MOC 2.0 progress was discussed with a focus on HIPPA/NIST 81 compliance requirements and urgent need for AI agent coding policies.

Metal Cube Team Alignment
The Metal Cube team is interested in becoming the default bare metal backend once development stabilizes but is not aligned with the group’s full use case for flexible backend configuration. Planning documents were provided by Dimmitri for review, scheduling a follow-up meeting for next Tuesday.

MOC 2.0 Compliance Needs
MOC 2.0 aims for a June 1st operational date, supporting a $50 million ARPA expansion and the State’s AI hub initiative, requiring strongly isolated clusters compliant with HIPPA and NIST 81. Expertise in regulatory compliance is being sought, with Scott Yoko and Red Hat consultants recommending design patterns.

AI Agent Usage Policy
The group decided to set a deadline two weeks out for agreeing on a minimum written policy concerning the use of AI agents for coding, recognizing that people are committing code without a policy which is dangerous. It was agreed not to forbid AI tools, but human review of pull requests must remain, and developers are responsible for their submissions.
